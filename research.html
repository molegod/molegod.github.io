research

My research interest is still not fully concrete (the case with all undergraduates applying for PhDs).

But generally, I am interested in:

Wearable AI systems that you can't distinguish from clothing or normal accessories.

  • Textile, strechable, morphing electronic interfaces.
  • How can we embed input and haptic devices into clothing or everyday wearables?
  • How can these devices be used for non-perceptible and seamless interactions?
    • Especially with computers and technologies like XR.

Non-traditional Computer or AI Input and Output.

  • EMG, BCI, gesture based controls that don't need traditional button inputs.
  • Non-traditional (eg. not text or audio) AI output like EMS, Olfactory, Temperature.
  • What can you do when you combine heavily integrated mechanical and electronical outputs?

Human Sensory and Nervous System Modulation

  • How do you augment humans by enhancing senses?
  • How do you combine pscyhology and perception in interactive computing?
  • How many haptic and sensory devices can I possibly attach to a XR headset to make it more realistic?
  • Can you make the human nervous system an output for AI?

Smart Computer Interfaces for Future Interactions (SciFi) Lab Cornell University

  • Embroidered textile electrodes, leading this project, currently paused due to summer REU. Also as I try and figure out how to do better electrical muscle stimulation and sensing.

Internet of Matter Lab University of Notre Dame

  • Currently (Summer 2026) working on a toolkit for electronic stretchable yarn.
  • Designing and fabricating various yarn primitives, e.g. microcontrollers, sensors, haptic devices, that can be connected to form an electronic yarn used to fabricate wearables.
  • Using a stretchable conductive silver ink printer and TPU based off of the Circuit2Yarn paper.

Human Augmentation in Physical and Perceptual Interactions (HAPPI Lab) Cornell University

  • Worked on a bunch of sensor electrode fabrication for an upcoming ICRA submission in Fall 2026 on characterizing electrode curvature. Will finish up in Fall 2026.
